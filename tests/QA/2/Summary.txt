SPD Comparison Summary

**General Domain and Structure**
The provided documents comprise Summary Plan Descriptions (SPDs) and Summaries of Material Modifications (SMMs) for four distinct multi-employer defined benefit pension plans serving culinary and hospitality workers in the Western United States. These documents are governed by ERISA and detail benefit calculations, eligibility, vesting, and administrative procedures. The SPDs serve as the primary rulebooks, while SMMs act as amendments updating specific provisions.

**1. Southern Nevada Culinary and Bartenders Pension Plan**
*   **July 2020 SPD:** The core document containing detailed Questions and Answers regarding participation, vesting schedules, benefit calculations, and appeals.
*   **2022 SMM:** A modification regarding COVID-19 assistance, automatically increasing 2020 work hours to 2019 levels to prevent breaks in service.
*   **2017 SMM:** An amendment announcing a 10% increase in benefit accruals and the removal of specific exclusions for disability pensions.
*   **Disability Notice:** A procedural notice outlining timelines for disability claims and appeals.

**2. UNITE HERE Northwest Pension Trust Fund (Seattle/Washington)**
*   **June 2005 SPD:** The foundational document describing benefits, specifically noting different rules for various local unions merged into the plan.
*   **2018 Notice:** Announces a change in administrative office to Northwest Administrators (NWA).
*   **2013 SMM:** Details a "Rehabilitation Plan" due to critical funding status, outlining preferred and default schedules for contribution increases and benefit adjustments.
*   **2010 SMM:** Notification of a reduction in future benefit accrual rates.
*   **2009 SMMs:** Notices regarding the addition of a 75% survivor option and temporary changes to accrual rates.

**3. San Francisco Culinary, Bartenders and Service Employees Pension Plan**
*   **December 2018 SPD:** A comprehensive document structured in three parts: a Q&A summary, the full legal Plan Document, and an ERISA supplement. It addresses the merger with the "Oakland Plan" and specific "Window" benefits.
*   **Window Benefit Notice:** An SMM detailing the "4-Year $50 Window VII" and "6-Year Window VII" benefits, offering enhanced rates for retiring within specific timeframes, alongside special COVID-19 vesting rules.

**4. Sacramento Independent Hotel, Restaurant and Tavern Employees Pension Plan**
*   **September 2016 SPD:** The primary guide for participants, featuring a checklist for retirement, a Q&A summary, and detailed articles on vesting, breaks in service, and reciprocity with other UNITE HERE plans.
